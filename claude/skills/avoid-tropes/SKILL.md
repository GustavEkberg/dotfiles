# Avoid AI Writing Tropes

When writing or editing any prose, copy, or blog content, read and internalize every trope in `app/thoughts/TROPES.md` before producing text.

## Process

1. Read `app/thoughts/TROPES.md` in full
2. Draft text while actively checking against every listed trope category
3. Self-review: scan output for trope clusters — any single pattern used once may be fine, but multiple tropes together or one repeated is not

## Quick Reference

The tropes file covers these categories — refer to it for full details and examples:

**Word choice** — no magic adverbs ("quietly", "deeply"), no "delve"/"leverage"/"robust", no ornate nouns ("tapestry", "landscape"), no "serves as" replacing "is"

**Sentence structure** — no "It's not X — it's Y", no "Not X. Not Y. Just Z.", no self-posed rhetorical questions, no anaphora/tricolon abuse, no filler transitions ("It's worth noting"), no trailing "-ing" shallow analysis, no false ranges, no gerund fragment litanies

**Paragraph structure** — no excessive short punchy fragments, no listicles disguised as prose

**Tone** — no "Here's the kicker", no "Think of it as...", no "Imagine a world where...", no false vulnerability, no "The truth is simple", no stakes inflation, no "Let's break this down", no vague attributions, no invented concept labels

**Formatting** — max 2-3 em dashes per piece, no bold-first bullets, no unicode arrows or smart quotes

**Composition** — no fractal summaries, no dead metaphors, no historical analogy stacking, no one-point dilution, no signposted conclusions, no "Despite its challenges..." formula

## Guiding Principle

Write like a human: varied, imperfect, specific. The tropes file is the authoritative reference — always read it for the full rules and examples.
